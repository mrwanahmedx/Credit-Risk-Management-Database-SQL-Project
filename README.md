# Credit Risk Management Database Project

Overview
- SQL-based relational database designed for banking credit risk analysis
- Models real-world banking entities and relationships
- Focuses on answering business and risk-related questions, not just data storage

Dataset
- Simulated banking data inspired by public Kaggle credit risk datasets
- Represents realistic customer, loan, payment, and credit behavior

Database Structure
- Customers: personal and demographic information
- Accounts: customer-linked banking accounts
- Loans: loan details, status, and exposure
- Payments: scheduled vs actual payments
- Credit Scores: historical credit score tracking
- Risk Assessments: internal customer risk classification

Business Use Cases
- Identify customers with late or missed payments
- Track active, paid, and defaulted loans
- Monitor credit score improvement or deterioration
- Detect high-risk customers with ongoing exposure
- Analyze outstanding balances and repayment performance

Skills Demonstrated
- Relational database design and normalization
- Primary and foreign key relationships
- Complex SQL joins and subqueries
- Aggregations for credit risk analysis
- Business-oriented querying and reporting

Tools
- SQL (MySQL / PostgreSQL / SQLite compatible)
